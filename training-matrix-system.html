What is a training matrix system?

A training matrix system helps a company manage training requirements, employee competencies, completed training, induction records, certificates, expiry dates and refresher training in one structured place. It gives the business a clear view of who is trained, who still needs training and which records are expiring.

In a SHEQ environment, training records are not only HR documents. They are part of safety files, legal compliance, risk assessments, inspections, contractor management and audit evidence. A system like eSHEQ Comply helps make training records easier to control and easier to prove when required.

Why training matrix control matters

  • Employees must be competent for the work they perform.
  • Training certificates and licences can expire.
  • Inductions and refresher training need proper records.
  • Audits often require proof of training and competency.
  • Contractors may need training evidence before site work starts.

Manual training files vs a digital training matrix

Many companies keep training records in folders and use spreadsheets to track expiry dates. This can become difficult when the company has many employees, contractors, job roles, certificates and refresher training dates. Files may be missing, certificates may expire and audits may become stressful because evidence is not easy to find.

A digital training matrix system keeps the requirement, employee record, evidence and expiry date together. It helps the team see which training is completed, which training is missing and which records need renewal.

Digital training matrix benefits

  • Less searching through old employee folders.
  • Clearer visibility of training gaps and expired certificates.
  • Better control over inductions, competencies and refresher training.
  • Improved evidence for audits, safety files and legal compliance reviews.
  • More structured employee and contractor training history.

What is a training matrix system?

Yes. eSHEQ Comply can support employee training records, certificates, inductions, competencies, expiry dates and contractor training evidence.

What should a training matrix include?

A training matrix should include employees, job roles, required training, completed training, certificate dates, expiry dates and evidence files.

Why track training expiry dates?

Expiry tracking helps prevent certificates, licences, inductions and refresher training from becoming outdated without follow-up.

How does training link to risk assessments?

Risk assessments often identify training requirements needed to control hazards and ensure people understand safe work methods.

How does training link to audits?

Audits often check whether employees and contractors are trained, competent and able to provide evidence for required work activities.
